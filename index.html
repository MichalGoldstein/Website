                    <p>Hi! I am Michal Goldstein, a PhD candidate in Music Technology at NYU. </p>

                    <p>My research focuses on the intersection of <b>music, cognitive science, and machine learning</b>. </p>

                    <p>At the Music and Audio Research Laboratory, I employ <b>computational methods</b> to investigate human perception and response to music, aiming to uncover underlying patterns in melodic structure and emotional expression.</p>

                    <p>My academic background, encompassing an undergraduate degree in Psychology and Musicology and a Master's degree in Musicology and Cognitive Sciences from The Hebrew University of Jerusalem, has prepared me for my current research at NYU. I am particularly interested in exploring the <b>convergence of music and technology</b>.</p>

                    <p>In addition to my research, I am dedicated to <b>teaching and mentorship</b>. As an adjunct professor and teaching assistant, I have guided students in music cognition, keyboard harmony, and research methodologies, fostering a collaborative and critical thinking environment.</p>

                    <p>I invite you to explore my work and engage in the exciting dialogue between <b>music, the brain, and technology</b>.</p>
